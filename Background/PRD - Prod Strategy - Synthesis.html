The Playing to Win framework by Roger Martin and A.G. Lafley transforms broad research into actionable strategy through a cascading set of five integrated choices [3] [4]:

  1. Winning Aspiration ‚Äî Define what winning looks like (for Frontera: becoming the leading enterprise platform for UK wealth management firms)
  2. Where to Play ‚Äî Narrow broad market research into specific choices: which customer segments, geographies, channels, and product categories [3]
  3. How to Win ‚Äî Identify your competitive advantage in chosen spaces (cost leadership or differentiation)
  4. Capabilities Required ‚Äî Determine what activities and competencies must exist to execute
  5. Management Systems ‚Äî Design systems to support and measure the strategy

Applying to Frontera: Take your disparate research on UK wealth management firms and filter it through "Where to Play" choices first ‚Äî which firm sizes, which specific pain points (compliance, client reporting, portfolio management), which buyer personas. Then assess "How to Win" against competitors like Intelliflo or SS&C. The framework forces you to make explicit trade-offs rather than trying to be everything to everyone [5].

Based on your Frontera documentation, here's how to integrate Playing to Win more effectively:

1. Connect Research Pillars to PTW Choices Your three research pillars (Macro Market Forces, Customer Research, Colleague Research) should directly feed the PTW cascade:

  • Macro + Customer ‚Üí Informs "Where to Play" choices
  • Customer + Colleague ‚Üí Validates "How to Win" options
  • Macro + Colleague ‚Üí Assesses organizational readiness for capabilities

2. Strategic Bets as PTW Hypotheses Your existing Strategic Bets format ("We believe... Which means... So we will explore...") should explicitly map to:

  • Where to Play (market/segment choice)
  • How to Win (competitive advantage)
  • Capabilities required

3. Synthesis Engine Enhancement The Cross-Pillar Synthesis should generate 3-5 complete PTW cascades as outputs, not just opportunity maps‚Äîeach with testable assumptions per Roger Martin's "What Would Have to Be True" methodology.

4. Agent Coaching Integration The Product Strategy Agent should guide users through reverse-engineering: starting with aspirations, then stress-testing Where to Play and How to Win choices against research evidence.
