## 1. Feature Overview

### 1.1 What It Is

The Product Strategy 6-Pager is the culminating deliverable of the Product Strategy Agent journey. It synthesizes the entire coaching engagement — Discovery research, 3Cs territory mapping, strategic synthesis, and selected strategic bets — into a professional, structured 6-page document that a CPO can present to their board, leadership team, or stakeholders.

### 1.2 Who It Is For

- **Primary**: CPOs and Heads of Product who need a board-ready strategy document
- **Secondary**: VPs of Product who need to cascade strategy to their teams
- **Tertiary**: Strategy consumers (PMs, Engineering Leads) who receive the document as strategic context

### 1.3 Why It Matters

This is the primary tangible output that justifies the entire coaching engagement. Without a polished, comprehensive strategy document, users complete a coaching journey but have nothing shareable to show for it. The 6-Pager bridges the gap between "I did strategic work" and "here is our strategy."

### 1.5 6 Page Strategy Narrative guidance

The Strategy document should be primarily a written narrative, with only key images and graphs. Others should be in an appendix. It should folow best practice from the Amazon Working Backwards method in written narratives.

Understanding the Structure
At the high level, there are 7 sections included in the strategy document. This creates a well-rounded memo structure. Here’s a breakdown of its key sections:
1. Introduction
The introduction sets the stage for your memo. It should provide background information and clearly state the purpose of the document. This section should answer the following questions:
Page 1: Product Vision & Strategic Context Defining the Product Vision and the strategic focus
·        What is the winning aspiration for the business?
·        What macro trends, regulatory shifts, and customer needs/ trends are shaping the market?
·        How does this align with Company strategy?
1b. State of the Business and Lessons Learned 
Provide a snapshot of the current situation. This section should include relevant data, statistics, and a brief analysis of the current state. It helps set the context and allows readers to understand the baseline from which decisions will be made.
- How has our strategy performed What worked/ did not?
- What have we learned from the previous time period: 6m, 12m etc.

Page 2: Adviser Problems, Market Trends & Product Opportunities Insights from Market Trend Analysis
·        What are the trends in the Market
·        Summarise the 3Cs - Territory Mapping analysis and insights
·         What are the most pressing adviser pain points and unmet needs?
·          What have we learned from research, service data, and market benchmarking?
·         What product opportunities emerge from these insights into the Synthesis? Insights from discovery and adviser feedback
Page 3: Where We Play & How We Win Strategic choices and competitive positioning
·        Summarise the chosen Play to Win opportunities
·        What are the key product bets and strategic themes to 2030?
·        How do these align with our capabilities and ambition?
·        What are the key assumptions underpinning our strategy?
·        What are the biggest risks and how will we mitigate them?
·        What strategic tests or prototypes will validate our direction?
Page 4: Product Strategy & Roadmap Themes From vision to action – what we’ll build and why
·        What is our North Star and Primary OKRs
·        Tenets are the guiding principles and values relevant to the memo. These are the non-negotiable elements that underpin your approach and decision-making process. Clearly stating these principles ensures everyone understands the foundational beliefs driving the project.
·        What is the high-level roadmap and sequencing logic?
·        What will we not do
Page 5: Operating Model & Capability Build How we’ll deliver – teams, tech, and governance
·        What is our product operating model and team structure?
·        What capabilities (data, design, engineering) are critical to success?
·        How will we govern, measure, and iterate?
Page 6: Strategic  - 
This is the core of your memo, where you outline the steps needed to achieve the goals. 
Detail the strategic bets and the key principles to implement them. 
Be specific about the actions required, the timeline, and who is responsible for each task.

7. Appendix
Include additional data, charts, documents, and any other relevant material that supports your memo. This section allows you to provide detailed information without cluttering the main narrative.

Strategies to Create the Perfect Strategy Document
Writing Clearly and Concisely: A 6-pager memo thrives on clarity and conciseness. Here are some tips to ensure your writing is clear and effective:
Emphasize Narrative Style: A compelling narrative engages the reader and makes the information more digestible. Tell a story that logically progresses from the introduction to the conclusion. Avoid bullet points and fragmented ideas; instead, write full sentences and paragraphs that flow naturally.
Avoid Jargon: Use clear and simple language. Avoid industry jargon and technical terms unless absolutely necessary. If you must use specialized terms, provide brief explanations.
Maintain Logical Flow: Ensure your ideas are logically organized. Each section should build on the previous one, leading to a coherent and persuasive argument. Use headings and subheadings to guide the reader through your memo.

Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them
Even seasoned professionals can make mistakes when writing a 6-pager memo. Here are some common pitfalls and how to avoid them:
Overloading Information: Be selective about the information you include. Focus on what is essential to support your argument. Too much detail can overwhelm the reader and obscure your main points.
Neglecting Visuals: While the 6-pager memo emphasizes narrative, visuals can still play a supporting role. Use charts, graphs, and tables sparingly to illustrate key points. Ensure they complement the text rather than replace it.
Ignoring Feedback: Seek feedback from colleagues before finalizing your memo. Fresh eyes can catch errors, unclear sections, and areas that need more detail. Incorporate this feedback to improve the clarity and effectiveness of your memo. (This can be built into a later phase)
Enhancing the Impact of Your Memo
To maximize the impact of your 6-pager memo, consider these strategies:
Engaging the Audience: Write in a way that captivates your audience. Use anecdotes, relevant examples, and a conversational tone to make the memo more relatable. The goal is to keep the reader engaged from start to finish.
Focus on Actionability: Ensure your memo provides clear, actionable recommendations. Decision-makers should be able to understand not just what needs to be done, but how to do it. Provide concrete steps and assign responsibilities where possible.
Conclusion
Mastering the art of the 6-pager memo can significantly enhance your ability to communicate complex ideas effectively. By adhering to the structured format, writing clearly and concisely, avoiding common pitfalls, and focusing on engagement and actionability, you can create memos that drive informed and strategic decision-making.

## 4. User Journey / Workflow

### 4.1 Current Flow (Generate and Export)

1. User completes Discovery, Research, Synthesis, and Bets phases
2. In Bets phase, user uses checkboxes to select 3+ bets
3. Sticky `BetSelectionPanel` appears at bottom showing selected count
4. User clicks "Create Product Strategy Draft"
5. System calls `POST /api/.../bets/strategy-document/generate`
6. Claude generates narrative sections; structured sections assembled from data
7. Document stored in `strategy_documents` table
8. `StrategyDocumentPreview` modal opens showing page 1
9. User navigates pages using numbered buttons
10. User clicks "Export PDF" — PDF downloads

### 4.2 Updated Flow (with editing and regeneration)

Steps 1-7: Same as current flow.

8. `StrategyDocumentPreview` modal opens with branded, card-based layout
9. User navigates pages using labeled tabs: "Executive Summary | PTW Cascade | Strategic Bets | Portfolio | Next Steps"
10. **[NEW]** User clicks "Edit" icon on any narrative section to enter inline editing mode
11. **[NEW]** User modifies text and clicks "Save" to update the document in state
12. **[NEW]** Optionally, user clicks "Regenerate" button on a section to have Claude rewrite just that section
13. **[NEW]** System calls `POST /api/.../strategy-document/regenerate-section` with section_id and context
14. **[NEW]** User clicks "Save Draft" to persist all edits to `strategy_documents` table
15. User clicks "Export PDF" — exports the latest saved version

### 4.3 Version History Flow (P2)

1. User opens Strategy Document preview
2. **[NEW]** User sees "Version History" button in header
3. **[NEW]** Clicking shows list of previous generations with timestamps
4. **[NEW]** User can select a previous version to view or restore
